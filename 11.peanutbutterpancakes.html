Peanut Butter pancakes

Ingredients
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¼ cup buttermilk
  • 1 egg (seperated)
  • ¼ cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp butter (melted)
    • Method

    1. Gather the ingredients
    2. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda and salt
    3. In a small bowl or measuring cup, combine the buttermilk, egg yolk, peanut butter and melted butter.
    4. Pour the buttermilk mixture over the flour mixture and stir until just mixed. It’s okay if there are a few lumps (over mixing will cause your pancakes to be flat and dense).
    5. In a separate bowl, whip the egg whites until soft peaks form.
    6. Scoop half of the egg whites into the pancake mixture and fold it in using a spatula. Add the rest of the egg whites and fold it in again. Make sure not to fully incorporate the whites into the mixture as you want to retain the air in the eggs.
    7. Heat a medium skillet over a medium heat. Coat the pan with a pat of butter and drop ¼ cupfuls of batter onto the hot skillet or griddle. Cook, turning once, until cooked through, 2 to 3 minutes per side.
    8. While the pancakes are cooking, make the syrup. Combine the crunchy peanut butter and maple syrup in a bowl. Stir until smooth.
    9. Serve pancakes topped with peanut butter syrup and, if desired, chocolate chips and whipped cream.
