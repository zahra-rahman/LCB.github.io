Milk Bread

Frankie Donato

Ingredients
  • 1 ½ tsp fresh yeast, crumbled
  • 350g whole milk at 20°C
  • 10g golden or maple syrup
  • 250g plain white flour
  • 250g strong white flour
  • 1 ¼ fine sea salt
  • 25g warm melted unsalted butter
    • Method

    1. In a large bowl, whisk the yeast with the milk and syrup. Add the flours and the salt, squidge the lot together with your hands until you have a soft sticky dough and the flour and liquid have evenly combined.
    2. Pour over warm butter, and then squeeze this into the dough. Scrape any remaining dough from your hands, cover the bowl and leave for 10 minutes.
    3. Rub 1 tsp of olive or corn oil on the work surface and knead the dough for 10 seconds, ending with the dough in a smooth round ball.
    4. Wipe the bowl clean and rubwith 1 tsp olive oil, return the dough to it, cover and leave for a further 10 minutes. Repeat this light kneading twice more, at 10 minute intervals, then leave the dough for 30 minutes.
    5. Grease and flour a deep loaf tin (12x19cm). Divide the dough into two equal pieces and shape each into a ball.
    6. Drop them side by side into the prepared tin, cover with a cloth, and then leave to rise for 1 ½ hours, or until almost double in height.
    7. Preheat the oven to 210°C. Brush the top of the loaf with a little cream or milk, and bake for 15 minutes, then lower the heat to 180°C and bake for a further 25-30 minutes, or until the top of the loaf is a shiny dark brown, and the loaf has come away from the sides of the tin.
    8. Remove from the tin, and leave to cool on a wire rack.
