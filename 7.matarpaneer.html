Matar Paneer

Ingredients
  • 1 cup Paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 1 cup Garden Peas
  • 2 Diced tomatoes
  • 3-4 Cloves of garlic
  • 1 Small piece of ginger
  • 1 Teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 Black cardamom
  • 2 Green cardamoms
  • 2 Cloves
  • 1 Cinnamon Stick
  • 1 pinch of Asafoetida
  • 1 Teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 Green chili or to taste
  • 3 Tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
    • Method

    1. Cut a slab of cottage cheese into small squares
    2. Chop the onions and tomatoes and keep aside.
    3. Grind the ginger, garlic and chilies in a chopper and keep aside.
    4. If you are using frozen peas, soak them in hot water for 15-20 minutes.
    5. In a frying pan, put 2 tablespoons of oil and fry the cottage cheese cubes until slightly brown on all sides.
    6. Once brown, take the cheese out and put them in a bowl full of warm water. This will keep the cheese soft.
    7. In the leftover oil in the pan, add the cloves, cardamom, cinnamon and cumin seeds.
    8. Quickly, add asafoetida and turmeric to the above.
    9. Straight after this, add the chopped onions and the ginger, garlic and chili paste.
    10. Do take care not to burn the mixture and keep stirring in between.
    11. Stir this mixture, until the mixture turns brown and the raw smell is gone.
    12. Add the freshly or tinned chopped tomatoes to this mixture.
    13. Keep stirring it in between, until the tomatoes leave the oil and all ingredients are mixed properly.
    14. Now add the peas without water and mix it into the mixture in the pan. Put the lid on the pan and leave it on the slow fire for five minutes.
    15. Now add the cheese into the mixture and add 2 cups of warm water or left over water from the cheese.
    16. Mix it all up, then put the lid on the pan and leave it for 10 minutes on the medium flame.
    17. Lift the lid up and stir it all with a spoon.
    18. Matar paneer is ready to serve with cooked rice.
    19. *** Tip: Make sure there is not too much left over water in the pot, so the consistency needs to be on the thicker side.
    20. Also, you may like to add butter or ghee (clarified butter) while serving it in a dish.
